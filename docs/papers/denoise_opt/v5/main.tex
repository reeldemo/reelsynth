  \begin{abstract}
    When a single-period wavetable is played with phase wrap, a sample discontinuity at the
    period boundary injects a broadband transient that repeats at the fundamental.
    We repair this cycle-local seam artifact by baking seam-local operators into the period
    inside a \textbf{bake cell}: classical DualCosine crossfade, polish, FIR blends, and optional
    tiny residual networks, optionally mixed by MoE soft gates.
    Candidates are ranked by the prolonged residual score $R\in[0,1]$ (1~=~best), the clamped
    tiled RMS agreement between the baked multi-period waveform and a procedural ideal sibling
    that withholds the open-wrap cliff.
    \textbf{DenoiseOpt} is the residual-scored hybrid meta-search: GA tournament updates,
    PPO architecture proposals, optional PBT exploit--mutate, and MoE routing over bake cells.
    Studio-clean pairs are not required for every trial.
    On the frozen canonical sine{+}cliff holdout (seed $20{,}260{,}719$), a compact favorite reaches
    $R\approx 0.991$ versus DualCosine $R\approx 0.825$ ($\Delta R\approx{+}0.166$).
    Separately, a live GPU campaign at the $5{,}000$-iteration clean gate reports champion
    $R\approx 0.9909$ against DualCosine $\approx 0.8166$ on the runner geometry.
    Scope is cycle-local wavetable seam restoration.
  \end{abstract}
  \keywords{unsupervised learning $\cdot$ wavetable $\cdot$ wrap discontinuity $\cdot$ seam restoration $\cdot$ DenoiseOpt $\cdot$ residual score $\cdot$ neural architecture search $\cdot$ reinforcement learning $\cdot$ genetic algorithms $\cdot$ meta-learning}
